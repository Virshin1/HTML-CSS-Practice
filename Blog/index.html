Why Prof. Prasad is a great teacher (with corny jokes)

If you’ve ever stepped into a classroom expecting a dull lecture and walked out with sore cheeks from laughing — congratulations, you’ve probably attended one of Prasad Sir’s classes. Or, as we fondly call him, Paddy Sir.

Now, don’t let the nickname fool you. This isn’t some chill dude lounging on a beach in Goa with a coconut in hand. No, no. Paddy Sir is an educational tornado — equal parts mentor, meme enthusiast, and strict-but-lovable taskmaster.

🎭 The Classroom Comedian

His lectures are pure entertainment. Imagine walking into class, and instead of being bombarded with dry theory, you’re hit with meme references, witty one-liners, and the occasional impersonation of a confused student (you know who you are).

He’s the kind of teacher who can explain complex concepts using a “How it started vs. How it’s going” analogy. Whether it's circuits, code, or life — there’s always a humorous angle that somehow makes it all click.

You come for the learning, you stay for the laughs… and because, let’s be honest, you don’t want to miss whatever legendary thing he says next.

🧪 Practical Overload — in a Good Way

Paddy Sir believes in doing over droning. While some teachers hand out notes like flyers, he hands out tools and assignments that get you into the lab faster than you can say, “Is this going to be on the exam?”

If you skip a practical, you’ll experience what students refer to as “The Look” — a silent expression of disappointment more powerful than a failed code compile.

You might walk into class thinking you’ll chill, but you’ll leave having debugged three programs, helped a friend solder a wire, and accidentally burned your lab sheet (don’t worry, he’ll laugh first — then give you another one and extra work for the ‘experience’).

🔥 Strict but Sincere

While he’s everyone’s favorite for his humor and personality, make no mistake — Paddy Sir doesn’t go easy. Deadlines are real. Standards are high. Expectations? Even higher.

You may laugh in his class, but if your assignment isn’t ready or your practicals are incomplete, the jokes stop. Temporarily.

He might say something like, “This assignment isn’t late, it’s just extremely early for the next semester,” before reminding you to redo the entire thing.

🧠 More Than a Teacher

Here’s what really sets him apart: he’s more of a mentor than just a teacher.

You won’t just leave his class with better grades — you’ll leave with better confidence. He doesn’t just teach subjects, he teaches perspective. Whether you’re confused about your future or just need a little push, he knows exactly what to say (usually with a meme reference included).

He treats every student with the kind of respect and challenge that says: “I believe in you… but you better deliver.”

Final Thoughts

Prasad Sir — Paddy Sir — is a rare kind of teacher. The kind who makes you laugh, learn, and grow, all in the same class. He’s the reason we actually look forward to lectures. Not just for the academics, but for the experience.

So here’s to the man who taught us how to debug code, laugh through deadlines, and maybe — just maybe — become better people in the process.

P.S. Sir, if you’re reading this… yes, we’re working on the practicals. Right after this blog post. Promise.
